I am a postdoc at Stanford Intelligent Systems Lab (SISL) working with Mykel Kochenderfer. I received my Ph.D. from Mechanical Engineering at CMU in 2023, advised by Ding Zhao. In the past, I received my bachelor's degree from IE ITS, in Indonesia and master's degree from IOE at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor.

Much of my work combines machine learning and rare-event theories to efficiently simulate rare catastrophic events. The applications of this line of work include the accelerated safety testing of autonomous vehicles. Currently, I am working on a few AI for safety and sustainability projects, which merge efficient simulation framework with optimization and decision-making algorithms. Check out my research page for a brief summary. Feel free to reach out for potential collaboration. Here is the link to my CV.
