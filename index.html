I am currently a postdoc at the Stanford Intelligent Systems Lab (SISL), advised by Mykel Kochenderfer. I earned my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Carnegie Mellon University in 2023, under the guidance of Ding Zhao. Prior to that, I completed my Bachelor's degree at Institut Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember (ITS), in Indonesia and my Master's degree in Industrial and Operations Engineering at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor.

My research primarily integrates machine learning with rare-event theory to efficiently simulate rare catastrophic events. This approach is particularly useful in the accelerated safety testing of autonomous vehicles. Currently, I am engaged in several projects that aim to leverage AI for enhancing safety and sustainability. These projects combine an efficient simulation framework with optimization and decision-making algorithms. For a concise overview of my work, please visit my research page. I am always open to exploring potential collaborations, so feel free to contact me. Here is the link to my CV for further information.
